About

I am an Associate Professor at the School of Law, Renmin University of China. My research focuses on criminal law, empirical legal studies, and judicial behavior. I employ quantitative methods to study sentencing decisions and the functioning of criminal justice systems.

I hold a J.S.D. and LL.M. from the University of Chicago Law School, an MSc in Criminology and Criminal Justice from the University of Oxford, and dual bachelor's degrees (LL.B. and B.B.A.) from Tsinghua University. I am admitted to practice law in both New York State and China.

Research Interests

Primary Areas

  • Criminal Law and Criminal Procedure
  • Empirical Legal Studies
  • Sentencing and Judicial Behavior
  • Comparative Criminal Justice

Methodology

My work combines doctrinal analysis with empirical methods, including quantitative analysis of court decisions, statistical modeling of sentencing patterns, and comparative institutional analysis. I am particularly interested in how legal rules interact with institutional and social factors in practice.
